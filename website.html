Agriculture

Agriculture is the practice of cultivating crops, raising livestock, and producing food, fiber, medicinal plants, and other products used to sustain and enhance human life. It is one of the oldest and most fundamental activities of human civilization. Over time, agriculture has become more technology-driven, with advancements in machinery, irrigation systems, fertilizers, pesticides, and genetically modified organisms (GMOs). These technologies aim to improve crop yields, disease resistance, and overall efficiency. Due to concerns about environmental impact and long-term food security, sustainable agriculture practices have gained importance. These practices focus on maintaining soil health, minimizing chemical inputs, conserving water, and promoting biodiversity. Agriculture is a major source of employment, particularly in developing countries. It provides livelihoods for millions of people involved in farming, fishing, forestry, and related activities.

Crops

Agriculture involves the cultivation of various crops, including cereals (such as rice, wheat, and corn), vegetables, fruits, legumes, oilseeds, and more

Livestock

Livestock farming involves raising animals like cattle, poultry, pigs, sheep, and goats for meat, milk, eggs, wool, and other products.
